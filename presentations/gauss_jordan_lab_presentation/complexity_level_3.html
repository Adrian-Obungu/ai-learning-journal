Numerical Stability & Pivot Selection

At the highest level of implementation, we move beyond the basic algorithm to address the challenges of floating-point arithmetic. Small pivot values can lead to significant rounding errors, which amplify through successive row operations, potentially rendering the final solution inaccurate.

The primary strategy implemented here is Partial Pivoting. By swapping rows to ensure the largest possible absolute value is used as the pivot, we minimize the magnitude of multipliers and significantly improve the numerical stability of the Gauss-Jordan process.
