Introduction to Vectors: The Language of Data

Algebraic Definition & Notation

A **vector** is an ordered list of real numbers, typically written as a column vector. The order of entries is crucial, and the number of entries defines its dimension.

v = [v1, v2, ..., vn]

Example: A 2-vector in R2: [3, -1]

Example: A 3-vector in R3: [1, 0, 5]

Vectors are fundamental for representing data points, features, and directions in multi-dimensional spaces. In AI, every piece of information, from a word to an image pixel, can be a vector.

Geometric Interpretation & Operations

Geometrically, a vector can be visualized as a **directed line segment** (an arrow) starting from the origin. Its length represents magnitude, and its direction indicates orientation.

Vector Operations:

  • **Vector Addition**: Combining two vectors (e.g., force vectors). Geometrically, this follows the "head-to-tail" rule or the "parallelogram rule."
  • **Scalar Multiplication**: Scaling a vector by a real number. This changes the vector's length (magnitude) and can reverse its direction if the scalar is negative.

These operations allow us to manipulate and transform data in meaningful ways, forming the basis for many AI algorithms.
