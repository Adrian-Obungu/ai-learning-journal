# Literature Note - UMD Math 240 - Linear Combinations and Parallelograms (1.3 Part 2)

## Summary
This session introduces the critical concept of a **linear combination**, which is a weighted sum of vectors. It also bridges the algebraic definitions of vector addition and scalar multiplication to their **geometric interpretations** in $\mathbb{R}^2$, particularly through the **Parallelogram Law** for vector addition. This interplay between algebra and geometry is fundamental for understanding vector spaces.

## Key Concepts
-   **Linear Combination**: A vector formed by summing scalar multiples of other vectors. Given vectors $\vec{v}_1, \dots, \vec{v}_k$ and scalars $c_1, \dots, c_k$, a linear combination is $c_1\vec{v}_1 + c_2\vec{v}_2 + \dots + c_k\vec{v}_k$.
    -   The resulting vector is still in the same vector space (e.g., $\mathbb{R}^n$).
    -   The scalars $c_i$ are often referred to as "weights."
-   **Geometric Interpretation of Vectors in $\mathbb{R}^2$**: A column vector $\begin{bmatrix} a \\ b \end{bmatrix}$ in $\mathbb{R}^2$ is visualized as an arrow starting at the origin $(0,0)$ and ending at the point $(a,b)$. This distinguishes the vector from the point itself.
-   **Parallelogram Law for Vector Addition**: Geometrically, if two vectors $\vec{u}$ and $\vec{v}$ are placed tail-to-tail at the origin, their sum $\vec{u} + \vec{v}$ is the diagonal of the parallelogram formed by $\vec{u}$ and $\vec{v}$. This is equivalent to the "tip-to-tail" method of placing the tail of $\vec{v}$ at the tip of $\vec{u}$.
-   **Geometric Interpretation of Scalar Multiplication**: Scaling a vector $\vec{v}$ by a scalar $c$ results in a vector $c\vec{v}$ that lies on the same line as $\vec{v}$ but with a length scaled by $|c|$. If $c$ is negative, the direction is reversed.
